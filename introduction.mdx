## What is the 1930 Helpline Platform?

The **CyberCrew 1930 Helpline Platform** is a digital solution developed for the Rajasthan Police Hackathon to modernize India's national cybercrime helpline (1930). The platform provides end-to-end management of online financial fraud complaints, connecting victims, police officers, banks, and courts in a single coordinated workflow.

The system combines a React frontend, a Node.js/Express backend, MongoDB database, and Python ML models to automate fraud detection and streamline the complaint lifecycle from filing through resolution.

## How it works

The platform follows a structured complaint lifecycle:

1. **Victim files a complaint** — submits fraud details, selects category/subcategory, uploads proof, and provides suspect identifiers.
2. **ML models analyze the case** — phishing detection and fraud legitimacy models automatically score the submission.
3. **Police review and verify** — assigned officers examine the complaint and mark it verified or rejected.
4. **Bank processes de-freeze** — if money is involved, the relevant bank receives a de-freeze request and processes account unlocking.
5. **Court takes judicial action** — courts access verified case files for formal legal proceedings.
