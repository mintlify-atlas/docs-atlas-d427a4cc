## Overview

The **Bank** role is for bank officials who handle the financial side of verified cybercrime complaints. The Bank dashboard has three views: the main complaints queue for reviewing police-verified cases where money was lost (`bankbool = true`), a status view for all processed cases, and a dedicated de-freeze section for processing account unlock requests after a court decision.

---

## Accessing the dashboard

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign in">
    Go to `/login`, enter your credentials, and click **Sign In**. The application detects your role as `Bank` and renders the Bank dashboard.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose a view">
    The menubar offers three views:

    - **Dashboard** â€” the complaints queue for cases pending bank action (default view)
    - **Status** â€” all complaints processed by the bank
    - **Defreeze** â€” the de-freeze request queue for court-approved cases

</Steps>

## Reviewing fraud complaints

The default dashboard view fetches all complaints that are police-verified and involve financial loss (`GET formupload/downloadformsnotverify`). Each complaint card shows:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Basic complaint details">
    - Application number (`applicationNo`)
    - Victim email address
    - Category (`category`) and subcategory (`subcategory`)
    - Incident date (`date`)
    - Delay in reporting (`delay`)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Bank details">
    Shown when `bankbool` is `true`:

    - Bank name (`bankname`), IFSC no. (`ifsc`), account no. (`accno`)
    - Transaction ID (`transactionID`), amount, reference no. (`refno`), transaction date
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Email compromise details (when applicable)">
    Shown only for the **Business Email Compromise/Email Takeover** subcategory:

    - Email taken by (`emailtakenby`)
    - Full email header (`header`)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Incident location and proof">
    - Platform where the incident occurred (`incidentoccur`)
    - Suspect identifier (`whereid`)
    - Uploaded proof image â€” click to open the full-size version in a new tab
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="ML prediction and police verification">
    - **Our Prediction** (`legitment`) â€” the ML model's fraud score for this complaint
    - **Police Verification** (`verifybypolice`) â€” the police officer's decision on this case
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Processing the account freeze decision

Each complaint in the queue has an **Account Freeze Status** control at the bottom of its card.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review the complaint">
    Examine the complaint details, bank transaction information, proof image, and police verification status.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select a freeze decision">
    Under **Account Freeze Status**, select:

    - **Yes** â€” freeze the account associated with this complaint
    - **No** â€” do not freeze the account

    The radio input name is keyed to the application number: `verify_[applicationNo]`.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Submit the decision">
    Click **Submit**. The platform sends `POST formupload/verifybypolice` with the payload:

    ```json payload
    {
      "verifybypolice": "yes" | "no",
      "applicationNo": "[applicationNo]"
    }
    ```

    After submission, the complaints list refreshes by re-fetching `GET formupload/downloadformsnotverify`.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Processing de-freeze requests

After the court issues a `yes` decision (`verifybycourt = yes`) on a complaint where money was lost, the case moves to the de-freeze queue. Switch to the **Defreeze** view in the menubar to process these requests.

The Defreeze view itself contains another menubar with two sub-views:

- **Dashboard** â€” de-freeze requests pending action (`GET formupload/downloadformsnotverifyfordefreeze`)
- **Status** â€” all de-freeze requests and their outcomes (`GET formupload/downloadformsfordefreeze`)

### Approving a de-freeze request

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review the de-freeze request">
    Each card in the de-freeze queue shows the full complaint details plus the police verification status and the ML prediction. Confirm the court decision has been recorded before proceeding.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select a de-freeze decision">
    Under **Account Freeze Status**, select **Yes** to approve the de-freeze or **No** to deny it.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Submit the decision">
    Click **Submit**. The platform sends `POST formupload/defreezebybank` with the payload:

    ```json payload
    {
      "verifybypolice": "yes" | "no",
      "applicationNo": "[applicationNo]"
    }
    ```

    The queue refreshes after submission.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### De-freeze status fields

The **Status** sub-view within Defreeze shows each processed de-freeze request with the following fields:

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| `verifybypolice` | Police verification decision |
| `verifybybank` | Bank's account freeze decision |
| `verifybycourt` | Court's judicial decision |
| `defreezestatus` | Bank's de-freeze approval or denial |

<Warning>
  The de-freeze queue only appears for complaints where `bankbool` is `true` and `verifybycourt` is `yes`. Complaints without financial loss or without a court decision do not generate de-freeze requests.
</Warning>
